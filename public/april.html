Thrissur Pooram

Date: April 20th

Location:Trissur

Description: A resplendent festival celebrated with a grand display of caparisoned elephants, dazzling parasols, and percussion music, the Thrissur Pooram is a magnificent spectacle merging the spiritual and cultural essence of Kerala. Celebrated in the Malayalam month of medam (April-May), the pooram is held at the Thekkinkadu Maidanam in Thrissur. Considered to be the mother of all poorams, this yearly temple festival was the brain child of Shakthan Thampuran, the Maharaja of Kochi, who organised the festival with the participation of 10 temples (Paramekkavu, Thiruvambadi Kanimangalam, Karamucku, Laloor, Choorakottukara, Panamukkampally, Ayyanthole, Chembukkavu, Neythilakavu).

Modes

By Air: The nearest airport to Thrissur is the Cochin International Airport (COK), located about 55 kilometers away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or use public transportation like buses to reach Thrissur.

By Train: Thrissur has its own railway station (station code: TCR) which is well-connected to major cities across India. You can check train schedules and book tickets to Thrissur Railway Station. From the station, you can take a taxi or an auto-rickshaw to reach the festival venue.

By Road: If you are traveling from nearby cities or towns, you can reach Thrissur by road. Kerala State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) operates buses to Thrissur from various parts of Kerala and neighboring states.

You can also consider hiring a taxi or driving your own vehicle to reach Thrissur. The city is well-connected by roads.

Local Transportation: Once you reach Thrissur, you can use auto-rickshaws, taxis, or local buses to get around the city and reach the Vadakkunnathan Temple, where the Thrissur Pooram festival takes place.
