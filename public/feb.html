Goa Carnival

Date: February 11th-14th

Location:Goa

Description: Carnival in Goa, also called "Carnaval", "Intruz", "Entrado", or (colloquially) "Viva Carnival" refers to the festival of carnival, in the Indian state of Goa. Though significantly smaller than the well-known Rio Carnival or the Portuguese Carnival of Madeira, the Goa Carnival is the largest in India and one of the few traditional celebrations of the Western Christian holiday in Asia. The current version of the Goa Carnival was modelled after the Rio Carnival by a local musician named Timoteo Fernandes and imposed in 1965 to attract tourists. It has since turned into a major tourist attraction for the small state.

Modes

By Air: Goa International Airport (Dabolim Airport): This is the primary airport in Goa and is well-connected to major cities across India and some international destinations. You can fly directly to Goa from cities like Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, and more.

By Train: Konkan Railway: Goa is well-connected by train through the Konkan Railway network. Major railway stations in Goa include Madgaon (Margao), Vasco da Gama, and Thivim. Trains from cities like Mumbai, Pune, Delhi, Bangalore, and Hyderabad operate to these stations.

By Road: Bus: Goa has a good network of state-run and private buses connecting it to nearby cities and states. You can take a bus from major cities like Mumbai, Pune, Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Mangalore to reach Goa. Sleeper buses and luxury coaches are available for overnight journeys. Car/Taxi: Goa is accessible by road from neighboring states like Maharashtra and Karnataka. You can drive to Goa via well-maintained national highways. Many people also opt for self-drive or hire taxis from cities like Mumbai, Pune, or Bangalore.

By Sea: Ferry: If you're traveling from Mumbai, you can opt for a unique sea journey by taking a ferry from Gateway of India to Mandwa Jetty (Alibaug) and then drive down to Goa. However, this is a longer and less common route compared to air or rail travel.

Local Transport: Taxis and Auto-rickshaws: Within Goa, taxis and auto-rickshaws are readily available for local transportation between towns and tourist spots. Negotiate fares beforehand, especially for longer journeys.

Renting a Scooter/Car: Renting a scooter or car is a popular choice for exploring Goa independently. Many rental agencies offer vehicles at reasonable rates.
