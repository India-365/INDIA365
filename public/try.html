Makar Sankranti / Pongal

Date: January 14th or 15th

Description: Makar Sankranti is a popular Hindu festival celebrated in January to mark the transition of the Sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn. It is a harvest festival, expressing gratitude for the winter crop. In South India, it's known as Pongal, with rituals like cooking a special dish called Pongal made from newly harvested rice and milk. The festival is also known for kite flying in some regions.

Makar Sankranti is a joyous occasion that brings communities together to celebrate nature, harvest, and the changing seasons.
